Project 1: Laser Cutting and 2D Design

Cardboard Pencil Holder

This week, I focused on 2D design, operating a laser cutter, and creating a press fit sculpture using identical individual parts. To start, I'll begin talking through my design process for the press fit sculpture. Now, originally, when I toyed around with Fusion360 and the laser cutter, I created this ninja star shape that I really liked a lot. I even cut 2 of them and tried fitting them together:



The problem with this is that the 2 ninja stars didn't fit together because I made the notches larger than the thickness of the cardboard which is 4mm. At any rate, I decided I didn't really like the ninja star design as much and tried creating something a little bit different with circles. The second design looks more like a star of some sort and it seemed promising as well:



Even though it looked super cool, this time, the notches were too short and the press fit wouldn't actually fit together. So, instead of adjusting the notches a little bit, I decided to scrap the star design all together in hopes of creating a foolproof design that even I couldn't screw up. So, I made a hexagonal shape that I turned into a snowflake, and this idea turned out better than I could have expected!



I am very proud of what I accomplished with the laser cutter, and I even plan to bring the pencil holder to the Cambridge After School Program for the kids to store their colored pencils in. Along with the laser cutter, I also experimented a little bit with the Fusion360 software to replicate a tape roll and screwdriver, to scale.
