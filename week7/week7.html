Project 7: CNC Milling

Gummy Bear and Play Button

This week, I was tasked with designing and producing something using CNC milling. I decided that the ShopBot was very useful, so I chose that to do the heavy lifting for me. For my first stab at the ShopBot, I designed a gummy bear that I could cut out of wood:


I wasn’t content with my gummy bear. It was too small, and I returned to the drawing board to make my design more complex. So, for round two of CNC milling, I designed a YouTube play button in Fusion360 and cut it out using the ShopBot:


My wooden cut out looked decent, so I went to the vacuum sealer to create a mold of it so I could cast it with plaster. The vacuum sealer was not a friend of mine this week, and thought it would be funny if it didn’t cooperate with me. It took me three different tries to get a decent casting, and it still was far from perfect:


Nonetheless, I mixed up some light blue plaster and poured it into the plastic mold so it would harden. The final product was very brittle, very ugly, and a good representation of “square one.”


I returned to the lab a couple of days later to redesign the YouTube button. I made the triangle cut a little deeper than what it previously was and used Kassia’s expertise to perfect the vacuum sealed mold. This time, the mold turned out perfectly and I made a similar plaster mixture with the same colors and added splotches of orange on the back to look like abstract goldfish.



Once the new YouTube button set, I was very pleased with the finished product and plan to give it to my brother for Christmas.
